On the other hand, the MLive.com website is Michigan’s most-read news site. MLive journalists publish hundreds of stories and photos every week.

I’ve mentioned previously that the online newspaper is essentially the same printed newspaper you are used to, but you read it on your computer, phone or tablet. It looks the same. You can flip through the pages the same way, on your electronic device of choice.

 

It’s the same favorite daily newspaper, but with a whole bunch of extra options included because the content isn’t limited to paper. And it’s only available to paid subscribers.

 

On the other hand, the MLive.com website is Michigan’s most-read news site. MLive journalists publish hundreds of stories and photos every week.

 

Many of those articles are free to non-subscribers. Some of them are Subscriber Exclusive – only available to paid subscribers.

 

The site is separated into the statewide homepage, and six regional homepages that correspond with your local newspaper’s coverage area. Each of those is broken down by topic for Michigan or the respective region – News, Sports, Weather, Tigers, Lions, etc. 

 

MLive homepages start at the top with Top Stories, followed by News, Sports and Life, as you scroll down.

 

Many of the stories in your online and print newspaper are written by those same MLive journalists. That’s one way your online newspaper and MLive are connected. (Check out Letter from the Editor on today’s Opinion page to find out about some new tools those reporters are using to enhance their journalism.)

 

A couple of weeks ago I introduced you to another connection between the two worlds. I told you about Top Stories in the online newspaper. Click Top Stories to get all the features and breaking news of MLive in the same easy-to-use online newspaper format you are used to. No need to navigate the complexities of the MLive.com website anymore.

 

Top Stories in your online newspaper corresponds with Top Stories on MLive. You get all of MLive in Top Stories, including those Subscriber Exclusives, and it’s all included in your newspaper subscription. You are getting all the stories an MLive paid digital subscriber would get at no extra cost. 

 

Why include MLive in the online newspaper? That’s because your newspaper is complete when you receive it – once we publish today’s paper, the news inside cannot change. But news happens all day long, and Top Stories changes constantly throughout the day. As news breaks, you can find it in Top Stories – all without leaving the online newspaper portal.

 

You might see a pop-up window reminder when you open the newspaper on your computer. In part, it says: Get the latest news delivered all day long included with your newspaper right at your fingertips.

 

Click on that pop-up or click on the Top Stories tab and add some MLive to your daily newspaper reading. You have everything to gain and it’s “right at your fingertips.”

On the other hand, the MLive.com website is Michigan’s most-read news site. MLive journalists publish hundreds of stories and photos every week.

 

Many of those articles are free to non-subscribers. Some of them are Subscriber Exclusive – only available to paid subscribers.

 

The site is separated into the statewide homepage, and six regional homepages that correspond with your local newspaper’s coverage area. Each of those is broken down by topic for Michigan or the respective region – News, Sports, Weather, Tigers, Lions, etc. 

 

MLive homepages start at the top with Top Stories, followed by News, Sports and Life, as you scroll down.

 

Many of the stories in your online and print newspaper are written by those same MLive journalists. That’s one way your online newspaper and MLive are connected.
